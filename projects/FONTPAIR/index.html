Typography Tips

Best Practices for Font Pairing

1. Contrast is Key: Pair fonts with distinct characteristics. Combine a serif with a sans-serif, or a decorative font with a simple one.

2. Limit Your Fonts: Use no more than 2-3 fonts in a single design to maintain visual harmony.

3. Consider Readability: Body text should be highly readable. Sans-serif fonts are often better for body text on screens.

4. Maintain Hierarchy: Use font size, weight, and style to create clear visual hierarchy.

5. Test at Different Sizes: Make sure your font pairing works well at various sizes, from headlines to captions.

6. Check Color Contrast: Ensure there's sufficient contrast between text and background for accessibility.

7. Consider Brand Personality: Choose fonts that reflect the tone and personality of your brand or project.

Font Categories

Serif Fonts: Have small lines or strokes at the ends of characters. Often perceived as traditional, respectable, and trustworthy. Good for print and headings.

Sans-serif Fonts: Lack the small strokes at character ends. Clean, modern, and highly readable on screens. Excellent for body text.

Display Fonts: Decorative fonts designed for use at large sizes. Use sparingly for logos, headings, or special elements.

Monospace Fonts: All characters have the same width. Often used for code, technical documents, or to create a retro feel.
