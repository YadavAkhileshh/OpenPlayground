Traffic Rules

Essential rules for safe driving and pedestrian behavior

Obey Traffic Signals

Always stop at red lights and stop signs. Proceed with caution at yellow lights, and only go when the light is green. Pedestrians should only cross at designated crosswalks.

Speed Limits

Always adhere to posted speed limits. Speed limits are set for optimal safety based on road conditions, traffic, and pedestrian activity in the area.

Seat Belts

Wear seat belts at all times when in a moving vehicle. Seat belts reduce the risk of fatal injury by 45% and moderate-to-critical injury by 50%.

No Distractions

Avoid using mobile phones while driving. Texting while driving increases the risk of a crash by 23 times. Keep your focus on the road at all times.

Don't Drink & Drive

Never drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs. Alcohol impairs judgment, reduces reaction time, and increases the risk of accidents significantly.

Pedestrian Right of Way

Drivers must yield to pedestrians at crosswalks and intersections. Pedestrians should make eye contact with drivers before crossing to ensure they are seen.

Safety Tips

Do's and Don'ts for different road users

Do's for Pedestrians

  • Always use designated crosswalks and pedestrian crossings
  • Look both ways before crossing the street
  • Make eye contact with drivers to ensure they see you
  • Walk on sidewalks whenever available
  • Wear reflective clothing when walking at night
  • Obey pedestrian signals and traffic lights

Don'ts for Pedestrians

  • Don't cross the road between parked cars
  • Don't assume drivers can see you
  • Don't use mobile phones while crossing the road
  • Don't cross when the "Don't Walk" signal is on
  • Don't walk on the road when sidewalks are available
  • Don't suddenly run onto the road

Do's for Cyclists

  • Always wear a properly fitted helmet
  • Use hand signals to indicate turns and stops
  • Ride in the same direction as traffic
  • Use bike lanes when available
  • Wear bright, reflective clothing at night
  • Check your bike before riding (brakes, tires, lights)

Don'ts for Cyclists

  • Don't ride against traffic flow
  • Don't use headphones while riding
  • Don't assume drivers can see you
  • Don't ride on sidewalks in business districts
  • Don't ignore traffic signals and signs
  • Don't carry passengers unless your bike is designed for it

Do's for Drivers

  • Always wear your seat belt
  • Obey all traffic signs and signals
  • Maintain a safe following distance (3-second rule)
  • Check blind spots before changing lanes
  • Yield to pedestrians at crosswalks
  • Use turn signals well in advance of turning

Don'ts for Drivers

  • Don't use mobile phones while driving
  • Don't drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs
  • Don't speed, especially in residential areas
  • Don't drive when tired or drowsy
  • Don't ignore weather and road conditions
  • Don't engage in aggressive driving behaviors
